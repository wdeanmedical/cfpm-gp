Client Rights and the Grievance Procedure

Personal Rights

  • You must be treated with dignity and respect, free from any verbal, physical, emotional or sexual abuse.
  • You may not be treated unfairly because of your race, national origin, sex, age, religion, disability or sexual orientation.
  • You may not be filmed, taped or photographed unless you agree to it.
  • You have the right to request information regarding the professional education and experience of the treating clinician.
  • You have the right to have all treatments explained to you/your child in a language and manner that is appropriate.

Treatment Rights

  • You must be allowed to participate in the planning of your treatment and care.
  • You understand that if you are enrolled in the EDT Program, family treatment may be indicated.
  • No treatment may be given to you without written, informed consent, unless it is needed in an emergency to prevent serious physical harm to you or other, or a court orders it. [If you have a guardian, however, your guardian may consent to treatment and medications on your behalf.]

Record Privacy And Access

  • Your treatment information must be kept private(confidential) unless the law permits disclosure.
  • Your records may not be released without your consent, unless the law specifically allows for it.
  • You may ask to see your records. You must be shown any records about your physical health or medications.
  • All sessions with GPS clinicians are confidential. Information shared in session will only be discussed with those with whom client has signed a release. If a client has chosen to invite other individual(s) into their session it is assumed that confidentiality is waived for individual(s) present.
  • Confidentiality is only required to be broken if in any of the following circumstances arise:
    • There is a clear and imminent danger of harm to yourself and/or others. GPS is required to report this information to the authorities responsible for ensuring your safety and the safety of others, family members, or potential targets of violence.
    • GPS learns of, or strongly suspects, physical or sexual abuse or neglect of a child, elder or disabled person. GPS is required to report this information to the appropriate state agency.
    • A judge issues a court order requiring GPS to release information contained in your records and/or requires GPS to testify in a court hearing.
